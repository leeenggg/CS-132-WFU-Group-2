Hi. We are Group 2.

And this is our project, Unequal Streams: Analyzing Access to Quality Water in the Philippines, which aims to analyze the state of water access and quality in the Philippines focusing on the differences between urban and rural communities.

It seeks to identify the political, economic, and environmental factors that affect people’s access to clean and safe water. By comparing the country’s situation with strategies used in other nations, the project hopes to highlight possible approaches that can improve water accessibility and management in the Philippines.

Here's an overview of our project.

Access to quality water remains uneven in the Philippines. Rapid urbanization, political issues, and natural disasters all affect the water quality in the country. Thus, this study aims to identify the effect of urbanization on water quality, quantify the accessibility of quality water between rural and urban communities, and identify the key limiting factors that hinder access to quality water.

Motivation

Access to clean and safe water is a basic human need, yet many Filipinos still struggle with limited or unsafe water sources. This issue inspired the study to better understand the factors affecting water quality and accessibility.

Problem

Unequal access to quality water persists across the Philippines, especially between urban and rural areas. Political, economic, and environmental challenges continue to hinder progress toward sustainable water systems.

Solution

The study analyzes current water access conditions and compares global strategies to identify practical solutions that can help improve water quality, availability, and management in the Philippines.

Research Questions

1

How has urban development affected the quantity of those who have access to quality water? How do rural and urban communities compare in terms of access to water?

2


What are the factors (political, economical, environmental) that obstruct people from quality access to water?

3


What edge do other countries have that allows or disallows access to quality water? Could these strategies be adopted by the Philippines?

Hypothesis

Main

Access to quality water in the Philippines is significantly influenced by factors such as urban development, geographic location, political conditions, and economic stability.

Null

There is no significant relationship between urban development, geographic location, and access to quality water in the Philippines.

Alternative

There is a significant relationship between urban development, geographic location, and access to quality water in the Philippines.
